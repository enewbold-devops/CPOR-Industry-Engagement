The intersection

Industry × Territory × Domain → Registration

A registration is never a generic statement. It is anchored to a specific industry code, a specific territory in the hierarchy, and a specific compliance domain — then verified against an authoritative source.

  • Names the exact law and the regulator that enforces it
  • Carries a risk rating, mandatory flag, and compliance deadline
  • Links to a citable legislation URL and a last-verified date
  • Approved by the Territory Manager who owns that jurisdiction

What changes for sellers

From a vague flag to an exec-ready fact

Before

"GDPR may apply"

No source, no date, no owner. The seller has to know the regulation themselves to ask the right questions — and can't cite anything in an executive conversation.

After

EU — GDPR (2016/679)

Names the regulator, the risk rating, the compliance deadline, the citable source, and the analyst and Territory Manager who verified it. Defensible and auditable.

Jurisdiction-aware by design

One engagement, one territory

A global customer like Toyota operates in many jurisdictions. The solution models each territory of operational impact separately, so the regulatory grounding a seller sees is always specific to the jurisdiction in play — never an averaged, generic blur.

  • Setting the industry on a Lead silently grounds it to the catalog
  • Qualifying the Lead carries that classification to the Account
  • Sellers do nothing extra — the intelligence simply appears
