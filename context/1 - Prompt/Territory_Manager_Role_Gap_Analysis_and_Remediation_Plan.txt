SECTION 1: ROLE EXPERIENCE SUMMARY
================================================================================

ROLE STRUCTURE
--------------
The current design defines ONE shared security role — "CPOR Global Industry Team"
— that covers all analyst-side personas. Three functional sub-roles are
distinguished by their primary work area in the app, not by separate security
permissions:

  Sub-Role              Design Term           Primary Work Area
  --------------------  --------------------  ----------------------------------
  Industry Analyst      Compliance Analyst    Registrations (RegulatoryRegistration)
  Industry Team Lead    Industry Specialist   Crosswalk (IndustryDomainMap)
  Data Steward          Data Steward          Taxonomy + Catalog Health Dashboard
  Territory Manager     Separate persona      Approval email (outside the app)

INDUSTRY ANALYST (COMPLIANCE ANALYST)
--------------------------------------
Daily work surface in the CPOR Industry Catalog app:
  - Active Registrations view (default) — sorted oldest-verified-first
  - Stale — Needs Reverification view — inline-editable cpor_lastverifieddate
  - Approaching Compliance Deadlines view — 90-day lookahead queue

New registration lifecycle via Business Process Flow (5 stages):
  Stage 1 — Draft              (Analyst: name, legislation name, legal type)
  Stage 2 — Taxonomy Validation (Analyst: industry code, territory, domain FKs)
                                 Context sub-grids show IndustryDomainMap and
                                 TerritorySourceMap for consistency validation.
                                 Warning shown if territory has no managerid.
  Stage 3 — Legislation Verification (Analyst: URL, risk, mandatory, lastverifieddate)
                                       RE-ENTRY POINT when Flow 1 recycles stale records.
  Stage 4 — Territory Approval (Territory Manager sign-off)
  Stage 5 — Activated          (System — Flow 4 advances automatically)

Automated support — Flow 1 (Stale Registration Weekly Digest):
  Every Monday: notifies Industry Team, sets stale Active records to Pending,
  moves their BPF back to Stage 3. Analyst does not need to hunt for aging records.

INDUSTRY TEAM LEAD (INDUSTRY SPECIALIST)
-----------------------------------------
Primary area: Crosswalk section of the site map.
  - Industry Domain Maps — maintains cpor_IndustryDomainMap records
    (industry code x compliance domain pairings)
  - Territory Source Maps — maintains cpor_TerritorySourceMap records
    (authoritative regulatory sources per territory)

Secondary: Catalog Health Dashboard (org-wide) — primary consumer for
coverage gaps and catalog-wide health signals.

Flows 2 and 3 (Lead auto-sync and Account industry code copy) operate
transparently under this persona's stewardship, maintaining catalog FK
integrity as sellers qualify pipeline.

Has Global Read on Lead, Account, Opportunity, Hypothesis for business context.
Does NOT have Write on engagement records.

TERRITORY MANAGER — CURRENT DESIGN
-------------------------------------
TM exists in the current design in exactly ONE functional capacity:
as an email-based approver (Flow 4). Their entire designed touchpoint:

  1. Analyst completes BPF Stage 3, sets cpor_registrationstatus = 154080003
     (Pending TM Review)
  2. Flow 4 detects status change, routes Power Automate Approval to
     territory.managerid of the registration's linked territory
  3. TM receives approval email, clicks Approve or Reject
  4. Optionally adds comments to cpor_regulatorynotes on rejection

The TM has no app role, no TM-scoped views, no dashboard, and no defined
channel back to Account Executives. Guide 07 flags a restricted read role as
optional and deferred. The design is approval-gated but NOT monitoring-capable.

SECTION 2: TM MONITORING — REQUIREMENTS AND GAPS
================================================================================

THE CORE REQUIREMENT
--------------------
The Territory Manager must:
  1. Monitor compliance risk and time-driven compliance obligations for their
     territories in the Industry Catalog
  2. Provide metrics and evaluations to Account Executives in the Sales CRM
     so AEs can act on compliance obligations relevant to their accounts

This requires three capabilities the current design does not have:

  1. VISIBILITY   — Territory-scoped catalog view (app role + scoped views)
  2. MEASUREMENT  — Risk + time metrics per territory (TM Health Dashboard)
  3. OUTBOUND SIGNAL — Metrics flowing to AE in Sales CRM (new Flow 5)

WHAT THE CURRENT DESIGN GIVES THE TM
--------------------------------------
  - cpor_stage4submitteddate field (Flow 4 Step 1b) stamps when analyst
    submitted for TM review — enables SLA tracking (7-day target)
  - A 7-day timeout and escalation in Flow 4 (Branch B escalation email)
  - cpor_approvedby and cpor_approveddate fields for approval record
  - Approval email payload includes: legislation, territory, risk, mandatory,
    applicability rule, legislation URL, and a direct record link

WHAT IS MISSING
---------------
  a) No TM security role — no app access defined
  b) No TM-scoped views (filtered to territory.managerid = current user)
  c) No TM Health Dashboard (territory-scoped parallel to global Catalog Health)
  d) No "Pending My Approval" queue — TM has no aggregate view of open approvals
  e) No outbound signal to AE — Flow 5 does not exist
  f) No re-verification context in approval email (is this new or re-verification?
     What changed since last approval?)
  g) cpor_xrm_client.js cannot identify the current user's systemuserid —
     getContext() returns only { userName, orgName, orgUrl }
  h) Zero role-based filtering in any webresource JS file

THE RISK + TIME SIGNAL MATRIX
-------------------------------
The most actionable intelligence a TM delivers to an AE combines
risk rating x time urgency. Four quadrant signals:

                         LOW URGENCY             HIGH URGENCY
                      (>90 days / stable)     (<90 days / stale)
                   +----------------------+----------------------+
  HIGH RISK        |  MONITOR             |  ESCALATE TO AE NOW  |
  (Mandatory)      |  Quarterly review    |  Flow 5 notification |
                   +----------------------+----------------------+
  LOW RISK         |  ROUTINE             |  REVIEW              |
  (Optional)       |  Annual review       |  Weekly TM digest    |
                   +----------------------+----------------------+

Current design handles ONLY the Stale -> Analyst path (Flow 1).
The ESCALATE TO AE NOW quadrant is entirely unaddressed.

TM APPROVAL QUEUE PROBLEM
--------------------------
The TM currently has no way to see all pending approvals in one place.
If a TM manages 12 territories and has 8 pending registrations, they have
8 separate email threads with no aggregate view and no SLA visibility.

Missing: A "Pending My Approval" view on cpor_RegulatoryRegistration:
  Filter:  cpor_registrationstatus eq 154080003 (Pending TM Review)
           AND territory.managerid eq [current user]
  Columns: Name, Territory, Risk Rating, Mandatory, cpor_stage4submitteddate,
           Days Pending (calculated: today minus cpor_stage4submitteddate)
  Sort:    cpor_stage4submitteddate ASC (oldest pending first)

RE-VERIFICATION HIDDEN WORKLOAD
---------------------------------
When Flow 1 recycles a stale Active registration back to Stage 3, it will
eventually return to Stage 4 — requiring TM re-approval. The approval email
currently looks identical for both new registrations and re-verifications.

Gap in Flow 4 approval payload: no prior approval history, no change delta
(e.g., "Risk Rating changed from Medium to High since last approval",
"cpor_lastverifieddate previously: 2025-12-01").

SECTION 3: OPEN QUESTION RESOLUTIONS
================================================================================

Q1: TM IDENTITY AND SCOPE
--------------------------
Question: Is the TM always territory.managerid? Multi-territory coverage?

Resolution:
  territory.managerid is the correct anchor. A TM managing multiple territories
  appears as managerid on each one. The scoping query pattern:

    Step A: GET /territories
              ?$filter=_managerid_value eq [currentUserId]
              &$select=territoryid,name
            → returns array of territory GUIDs the TM manages

    Step B: Filter registrations using OR chain on _cpor_territory_value:
            _cpor_territory_value eq 'id1'
            or _cpor_territory_value eq 'id2'
            or _cpor_territory_value eq 'id3'

  NOTE: Dataverse Web API does NOT support $in operator on lookup fields.
  An explicit OR chain is required. This pattern is already used elsewhere
  in the codebase.

  BLOCKER: cpor_xrm_client.js getContext() returns { userName, orgName, orgUrl }
  only. It does NOT return systemuserid. A new getCurrentUserId() function is
  required:
    Xrm.Utility.getGlobalContext().getUserId() — strip curly braces from result.

Q2: AE TO TM RELATIONSHIP
--------------------------
Question: How does the TM know which AEs work accounts in their territory?

Resolution:
  The join already exists in OOB D365:
    account.territoryid → territory.territoryid → territory.managerid = TM
    account.ownerid = AE

  Flow 5 walks this chain:
    TM's territories → accounts assigned to those territories
    → account.ownerid (AE systemuser) → create Task on Account owned by AE

  No new fields needed. This is entirely OOB relationship traversal.

Q3: METRICS CADENCE
--------------------
Question: Fixed cadence (QBR, weekly) or threshold-triggered?

Resolution: Two modes:
  a) WEEKLY AUTOMATED — same Monday recurrence as Flow 1, offset +2 hours
     so stale processing completes first. Creates Task records on Accounts
     in D365 Sales. AE sees Tasks in their Activity timeline and My Activities.

  b) ON-DEMAND — TM presses "Generate AE Report" command bar button in
     the TM Health webresource. Triggers an instant flow via HTTP POST.
     TM can fire this outside the weekly cadence when a critical regulation
     is activated or when a deadline crosses an urgency threshold.

Q4: TM WRITE SCOPE ON REGISTRATION
------------------------------------
Question: Which fields should the TM be able to write independently?

Resolution: TM writes EXACTLY four fields via Field Security Profile:
  cpor_approvedby       — signs the approval (Lookup to systemuser)
  cpor_approveddate     — timestamps the approval (Date Only)
  cpor_regulatorynotes  — jurisdiction caveats or rejection reason (TextArea)
  cpor_registrationstatus — sets Active (approve) or Pending (reject) (Choice)

TM must NOT write:
  cpor_riskrating, cpor_ismandatory, cpor_compliancedomain,
  cpor_industrycode, cpor_territory, cpor_legislationurl,
  cpor_legislationname, any taxonomy anchor field

Rationale: Risk rating and taxonomy classification are analyst responsibilities.
The TM's role is jurisdictional sign-off, not regulatory classification.

Implementation: Standard Dataverse security roles do not restrict write to
specific columns. A Field Security Profile named "CPOR TM Write Fields"
is required, granting Write on only the four fields above. Assign the
profile to the CPOR Territory Manager security role.

Q5: cpor_approvaldate DEPLOYMENT STATUS — CRITICAL NAMING CONFLICT
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Question: Is cpor_approvaldate deployed? Guide 07 says it is not yet deployed.

Resolution: CRITICAL DISCREPANCY FOUND.

The deployed schema file (cpor_regulatoryregistration.schema.json) shows
TWO fields already deployed that Guide 07 misidentifies:

  Deployed (Schema)    Guide 07 Says                    Status per Guide
  -------------------  -------------------------------  -------------------
  cpor_approvedby      cpor_verifiedby                  "IS deployed"
  cpor_approveddate    cpor_approvaldate                "NOT YET deployed"

Guide 07's field reference correction table even lists cpor_approvedby as
an "earlier incorrect name" for cpor_verifiedby — but the schema file proves
cpor_approvedby IS the actual deployed logical name.

CONSEQUENCE: Every Flow 4 step, BPF Stage 4 step, and form field that
references cpor_verifiedby or cpor_approvaldate is using the WRONG logical
name and WILL FAIL at runtime with a field-not-found error.

RESOLUTION: Trust the schema file.
  Deployed logical names are: cpor_approvedby and cpor_approveddate.
  All implementation guide references to cpor_verifiedby must be corrected
  to cpor_approvedby. All references to cpor_approvaldate must be corrected
  to cpor_approveddate. The field the guides say needs to be created
  ALREADY EXISTS under a different name.

VERIFICATION COMMAND (run before implementing):
  GET /EntityDefinitions(LogicalName='cpor_regulatoryregistration')
      /Attributes?$filter=startswith(LogicalName,'cpor')
      &$select=LogicalName,DisplayName
  Confirm cpor_approvedby and cpor_approveddate appear in the result.

Q6: LONGITUDINAL HEALTH TRACKING
----------------------------------
Question: How does the TM provide trend data (e.g., coverage improved 15%
this quarter) to AEs for QBR purposes?

Resolution: Defer cpor_CatalogHealthSnapshot entity to Phase 2.

For the initial TM experience, the weekly Flow 1 run history plus
cpor_approveddate timestamps provide sufficient point-in-time signal.

Phase 2 plan: Add cpor_CatalogHealthSnapshot entity (one row per territory
per weekly run). Fields: territory, active_count, stale_count, high_risk_count,
pending_tm_count, snapshot_date. Power BI embedded tile or MDA chart reads
this for trend lines over rolling 12 weeks.

GUIDE 08 (NEW): CPOR Territory Manager Security Role
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

PURPOSE:
  Define the CPOR Territory Manager security role, its privilege matrix,
  the required Field Security Profile, and how the TM gains access to
  the CPOR Industry Catalog app with a scoped site map area.

ROLE NAME: CPOR Territory Manager
DESCRIPTION: Grants Territory Managers read access to all catalog entities
  and write access to approval fields on cpor_RegulatoryRegistration only.
  Row-level scoping to the TM's territories is enforced at the view and
  webresource layer (see Guide 09), not at the Dataverse privilege layer.

PRIVILEGES ON CATALOG ENTITIES:

  Entity                       Create  Read    Write   Delete  Append  AppendTo
  ---------------------------  ------  ------  ------  ------  ------  --------
  cpor_RegulatoryRegistration  None    Global  Global* None    Global  Global
  cpor_IndustryCode            None    Global  None    None    None    None
  cpor_ComplianceDomain        None    Global  None    None    None    None
  territory                    None    Global  None    None    None    None
  cpor_RegulatorySource        None    Global  None    None    None    None
  cpor_IndustryDomainMap       None    Global  None    None    None    None
  cpor_TerritorySourceMap      None    Global  None    None    None    None

  * Write on cpor_RegulatoryRegistration is intentionally Global scope
    at the role level, but restricted to 4 specific fields via the
    Field Security Profile defined below.

PRIVILEGES ON ENGAGEMENT ENTITIES:

  Entity            Create  Read    Write  Delete
  ----------------  ------  ------  -----  ------
  lead              None    Global  None   None
  opportunity       None    Global  None   None
  account           None    Global  None   None

FIELD SECURITY PROFILE: CPOR TM Write Fields
  Purpose: Restricts the TM's Write privilege on cpor_RegulatoryRegistration
           to exactly 4 fields. All other fields on the entity remain read-only
           for the TM despite the Global Write on the role.
  Fields:
    cpor_approvedby         Allow Update: Yes
    cpor_approveddate       Allow Update: Yes
    cpor_regulatorynotes    Allow Update: Yes
    cpor_registrationstatus Allow Update: Yes

  PORTAL STEPS:
    In make.powerapps.com, navigate to Settings > Advanced Settings >
    Security > Field Security Profiles.
    Select New. Name: CPOR TM Write Fields.
    Add the 4 fields above. Set Allow Update = Yes for each.
    Assign the profile to: CPOR Territory Manager (security role).

SOFT-SCOPE APPROACH (Phase 1 Decision):
  This design uses UI-level territory filtering rather than Dataverse Owner
  Teams for row-level security. Advantages: minimal infrastructure change,
  no need to restructure record ownership. Limitation: a TM with direct API
  access (e.g., Power Automate) could technically read registrations outside
  their territories.

  Phase 2 upgrade path: Implement Owner Teams per Territory. Each
  cpor_RegulatoryRegistration record's ownerid is set to the territory's
  owner team. TM is a member of their territory's team. Switch TM role
  Write privilege from Global to Business Unit scope.

APP ACCESS:
  Add the CPOR Territory Manager role to the CPOR Industry Catalog app
  in the App Designer > Manage Roles dialog. The TM then sees the app
  in their list of available apps.

SITE MAP ADDITION — "My Territory" Area:
  In the App Designer, add a new Area to the CPOR Industry Catalog site map:
    Area:   My Territory
    Group:  Territory Health
    Pages:
      - TM Health (Web Resource: cpor/html/cpor_tm_health.html)
        [type: Web Resource — see Guide 09]
      - Registrations (Table: cpor_RegulatoryRegistration)
        Default view: set to the "Pending My Approval" view

  VIEW ADDENDUM — Pending My Approval:
    Entity:  cpor_RegulatoryRegistration
    Filter:  cpor_registrationstatus eq 154080003 (Pending TM Review)
             NOTE: Row filtering to current user's territories is enforced

    Columns: cpor_name, Territory, Risk Rating, Mandatory,

    Default: YES (for the My Territory > Registrations page)

    Entity:  cpor_RegulatoryRegistration

PORTAL STEPS:

GUIDE 10 (NEW): Flow 5 — Territory Compliance Health Report to AE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

PURPOSE:
  Weekly automated flow that creates Task records on Accounts in D365 Sales,
  surfacing compliance risk signals to Account Executives without requiring
  AEs to access the CPOR Industry Catalog app.

FLOW NAME:    CPOR — Territory Compliance Health Report to AE
TRIGGER TYPE: Recurrence (Scheduled)

TRIGGER:
  Frequency:  Week
  Interval:   1
  Days:       Monday
  Start time: 11:00 UTC
  (Offset +2 hours from Flow 1 at 09:00 UTC. Flow 1 stale processing
   completes before this flow queries active/stale data.)

ALSO TRIGGERABLE ON-DEMAND:
  Add an HTTP request trigger (Instant cloud flow trigger) in a separate
  instant flow. The TM Health webresource "Generate AE Report" button
  POSTs to this trigger URL. The instant flow calls the same logic as the
  scheduled flow, scoped to the requesting TM's territories.

STEP 1: Get all Territory Managers
  Action: List rows (Dataverse connector)
    Table Name:   territories
    Filter rows:  _managerid_value ne null
                  AND cpor_territorycode ne null
    Select columns: territoryid,name,_managerid_value
  Store as varTerritoryList.

STEP 2: Apply to each territory
  Input: varTerritoryList

  STEP 2a: Get compliance-relevant registrations for this territory
  Action: List rows (Dataverse connector)
    Table Name:   cpor_regulatoryregistrations
    Filter rows:
      _cpor_territory_value eq @{items('Apply_to_each')['territoryid']}
      AND cpor_registrationstatus eq 154080000
      AND (
        (cpor_ismandatory eq true AND cpor_compliancedeadline le
          @{addDays(utcNow(), 90)})
        OR (cpor_riskrating eq 154080000 AND cpor_lastverifieddate le
          @{addDays(utcNow(), -90)})
        OR (cpor_registrationstatus eq 154080000 AND createdon ge
          @{addDays(utcNow(), -7)})
      )
    Select columns: cpor_regulatoryregistrationid,cpor_name,cpor_legislationname,
                    cpor_riskrating,cpor_ismandatory,cpor_compliancedeadline,
                    cpor_lastverifieddate,_cpor_compliancedomain_value
    Order by: cpor_compliancedeadline asc

  STEP 2b: Check if any relevant registrations exist
  Action: Condition
    length(outputs('List_registrations')?['body/value']) Greater than 0
  False branch: Terminate (Succeeded) — no action needed for this territory.

  STEP 2c (True): Get Accounts assigned to this territory
  Action: List rows (Dataverse connector)
    Table Name: accounts
    Filter rows: _territoryid_value eq @{items('Apply_to_each')['territoryid']}
    Select columns: accountid,name,_ownerid_value,owneridname

  STEP 2d (True): Check if any accounts exist
  Action: Condition
    length(outputs('List_accounts')?['body/value']) Greater than 0
  False: Terminate (Succeeded) — territory has no accounts.

  STEP 2e (True): Apply to each account
    Input: accounts from Step 2c

    STEP 2e-i: Build compliance summary for this account
    Action: Compose — varComplianceSummary
      Content:
        CPOR Compliance Health — @{items('Apply_to_each_account')['name']}
        Territory: @{items('Apply_to_each')['name']}
        Report Date: @{utcNow('yyyy-MM-dd')}

        The following compliance obligations require your awareness:

        @{join(
          map(
            outputs('List_registrations')?['body/value'],
            item => concat(
              '• ', item['cpor_name'],
              ' | Risk: ', item['cpor_riskrating'],
              if(item['cpor_ismandatory'], ' | MANDATORY', ''),
              if(not(equals(item['cpor_compliancedeadline'], null)),
                concat(' | Deadline: ', item['cpor_compliancedeadline']),
                '')
            )
          ),
          decodeUriComponent('%0A')
        )}

        Review with your account team to confirm applicability.
        Open the CPOR Industry Catalog for full registration details.

    STEP 2e-ii: Create Task on Account
    Action: Create a new row (Dataverse connector)
      Table Name: tasks
      Row values:
        subject:                CPOR Compliance Health — @{items('Apply_to_each')['name']}
                                @{utcNow('yyyy-MM-dd')}
        description:            @{outputs('varComplianceSummary')}
        regardingobjectid_account@odata.bind:
          /accounts(@{items('Apply_to_each_account')['accountid']})
        ownerid@odata.bind:
          /systemusers(@{items('Apply_to_each_account')['_ownerid_value']})
        scheduledend:           @{addDays(utcNow(), 7)}
        category:               CPOR Compliance
        prioritycode:           2 (High)

  STEP 2f (True): Notify TM of report completion
  Action: Send an email (V2) or Teams notification
    To:      @{outputs('Get_Territory_Manager_Email')} (resolved from managerid)
    Subject: CPOR AE Report Generated — @{items('Apply_to_each')['name']}
    Body:    Compliance health tasks have been created for
             @{length(outputs('List_accounts')?['body/value'])} accounts
             in territory @{items('Apply_to_each')['name']}.
             @{length(outputs('List_registrations')?['body/value'])} compliance
             obligations were included. Tasks are due in 7 days.

GUIDE 11 (UPDATE): cpor_xrm_client.js — TM Identity and Territory Utilities
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

PURPOSE:
  Add four new utility functions to cpor_xrm_client.js enabling TM-scoped
  data access. These functions are required by Guide 09 (TM Health webresource)
  and available to any future TM-aware page.

PREREQUISITE: Existing cpor_xrm_client.js deployed and functioning.

NEW FUNCTIONS TO ADD:

FUNCTION 1: getCurrentUserId()
  Purpose: Returns the Dataverse systemuserid of the currently logged-in user.
           Used as the anchor for all TM scoping queries.
  Implementation:
    getCurrentUserId() {
        try {
            const raw = Xrm.Utility.getGlobalContext().getUserId();
            // Dataverse returns GUIDs wrapped in curly braces: {guid}
            return raw ? raw.replace(/[{}]/g, '').toLowerCase() : null;
        } catch (e) {
            console.error('[CporXrm] getCurrentUserId error:', e);
            return null;
        }
    }
  Note: Xrm.Utility.getGlobalContext() is available in both full page
  context and iframe context. If called outside an MDA context (e.g., in
  a standalone test), it will throw — the try/catch handles this.

FUNCTION 2: getManagedTerritoryIds()
  Purpose: Returns a Promise resolving to an array of territory GUIDs where
           the current user is the territory manager.
  Implementation:
    async getManagedTerritoryIds() {
        const userId = this.getCurrentUserId();
        if (!userId) return [];
        try {
            const result = await this.fetchRecords(
                'territories',
                `?$filter=_managerid_value eq ${userId} and cpor_territorycode ne null` +
                `&$select=territoryid,name` +
                `&$top=200`
            );
            return (result.records || []).map(t => t.territoryid);
        } catch (e) {
            console.error('[CporXrm] getManagedTerritoryIds error:', e);
            return [];
        }
    }
  Note: Filter includes cpor_territorycode ne null to exclude OOB sales
  routing territories. Cap at 200 — a TM managing more than 200 territories
  is an edge case that warrants a separate discussion.

FUNCTION 3: isTerritoryManager()
  Purpose: Returns a Promise resolving to boolean — true if the current
           user manages at least one CPOR territory.
  Implementation:
    async isTerritoryManager() {
        const ids = await this.getManagedTerritoryIds();
        return ids.length > 0;
    }

FUNCTION 4: buildTerritoryFilter(territoryIds)
  Purpose: Builds an OData filter expression scoping a query to the provided
           list of territory GUIDs via the cpor_territory lookup field.
  Implementation:
    buildTerritoryFilter(territoryIds) {
        if (!territoryIds || territoryIds.length === 0) {
            // Return a filter that matches nothing — safety guard
            return '_cpor_territory_value eq null';
        }
        if (territoryIds.length === 1) {
            return `_cpor_territory_value eq ${territoryIds[0]}`;
        }
        return territoryIds
            .map(id => `_cpor_territory_value eq ${id}`)
            .join(' or ');
    }
  IMPORTANT: Dataverse Web API OData does NOT support the $in operator
  on lookup fields. An explicit OR chain is required. Tested pattern:
    _cpor_territory_value eq 'id1' or _cpor_territory_value eq 'id2'
  For large territory sets (>20), wrap in parentheses and consider
  splitting into multiple queries to avoid OData filter length limits.

SECTION 7: ARCHITECTURAL DECISIONS LOG
================================================================================

DECISION 1: Soft-scope (view-level) vs. Owner Teams for TM row security
  Chosen: Soft-scope (Phase 1)
  Rationale: Owner Teams require restructuring record ownership (all existing
  registrations owned by analysts). Soft-scope achieves the UX goal with
  minimal infrastructure change. Row-level security is not the primary
  concern for Phase 1 — the TM has read access to the full catalog via their
  role, and the view filtering provides the operational scoping they need.
  Phase 2 path: Implement Owner Teams per Territory.

DECISION 2: Task entity (OOB) vs. new cpor_ComplianceAlert entity for AE signal
  Chosen: Task entity (OOB)
  Rationale: Tasks appear natively in the Account Activity timeline and AE's
  My Activities view with no configuration. AEs work in D365 Sales — they
  already use Tasks. A new entity would require form additions to the Sales app,
  additional security role changes, and change management for AEs.

DECISION 3: cpor_CatalogHealthSnapshot entity for trend tracking
  Chosen: Deferred to Phase 2
  Rationale: Point-in-time flow run history and cpor_approveddate timestamps
  provide sufficient initial signal. Historical trend analysis (QBR trend charts)
  requires the entity. Defer to avoid scope creep in Phase 1.

DECISION 4: Flow 5 cadence
  Chosen: Weekly (Monday 11:00 UTC) + on-demand HTTP trigger
  Rationale: Weekly aligns with the established operational cadence (Flow 1
  also runs Monday morning). On-demand allows TM to generate reports when a
  critical regulation is activated outside the weekly cycle.

DECISION 5: Trust schema file for cpor_approvedby / cpor_approveddate
  Chosen: Schema file is authoritative
  Rationale: Dataverse does not allow renaming a field's logical name after
  creation. If the schema file shows cpor_approvedby, that IS the deployed
  logical name. Guide 07's claim that cpor_verifiedby is deployed is incorrect.
  Verify with EntityDefinitions API before implementing Guide 12 corrections.

DECISION 6: cpor_stage4submitteddate — create vs. use existing date field
  Chosen: Create as new field
  Rationale: No existing date field on cpor_RegulatoryRegistration captures
  the moment of Stage 4 submission specifically. cpor_lastverifieddate captures
  the last analyst verification date, not the approval submission date.
  The SLA tracking requirement (7-day target from submission to TM approval)
  requires a distinct timestamp.
